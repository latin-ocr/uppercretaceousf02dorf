cies whose remains are most abundant and those whose remains are most persistently
present at the various horizons within the deposit. The most useful results of
quantitative studies are obtained from localities where several thousand specimens
can be collected.¹ Unfortunately‚ none of the localities in the Lance Creek area has
yielded more than 280 specimens. F ailure to secure large collections is a result of
the limited extent, both horizontally and vertically, of the lenticular beds containing
the plant remains.
